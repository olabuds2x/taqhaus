## Core Doctrine

### The Product Marketing Stack

Every product marketing decision operates within this hierarchy. Each layer depends on the one above it:

```
1. Market Understanding    → Who buys? What triggers purchase?
2. Positioning & Framing   → How does the market categorize you?
3. Messaging Architecture  → What do you say at each stage?
4. Buyer Journey Design    → What path do they walk?
5. Demand Generation       → How do they enter the funnel?
6. Sales Enablement        → What tools close the deal?
7. Revenue Attribution     → What's working? What's waste?
```

**Rule**: If Layer 1 is wrong, everything below it is noise. You cannot out-message a misunderstood market.

## Market Understanding

### The JTBD Framework (Jobs-To-Be-Done)

Prospects don't buy services. They hire solutions to do jobs:

```
When [SITUATION], I want to [MOTIVATION], so I can [EXPECTED OUTCOME].
```

**For a marketing consultancy like TaqHaus:**
- "When I'm scaling past $1M ARR, I want a strategic marketing partner, so I can focus on product while growth compounds."
- "When my current agency delivers reports but not revenue, I want someone who owns outcomes, so I can tie marketing spend to actual pipeline."
- "When I need a website that actually generates leads, I want engineers who think like marketers, so I can stop treating my site as a brochure."

### Purchase Trigger Mapping

Every ICP has specific triggers that move them from "aware" to "evaluating":

| Trigger | ICP | Urgency |
|---------|-----|---------|
| Competitor just launched a rebrand | Founders & CEOs | High — ego + fear |
| Board asks "what's the marketing strategy?" | Growth-stage SMBs | High — accountability |
| Current agency contract expires | All | Medium — natural switching window |
| Website redesign needed | Professional services | Medium — budget cycle aligned |
| Election season approaching | Politicians | Urgent — deadline-driven |
| Hired a sales team but no inbound leads | B2B companies | High — payroll pressure |

## Buyer Journey Architecture

### The 5 Stages of B2B Consultancy Buying

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STAGE 1: UNAWARE                                         │
│ "I have a problem but I don't know it's solvable"        │
│ Content: Blog posts, thought leadership, SEO articles    │
│ Channel: Organic search, LinkedIn, AI citations          │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ STAGE 2: PROBLEM-AWARE                                   │
│ "I know I have a marketing problem"                       │
│ Content: Pain-point articles, audit tools, diagnostics    │
│ Channel: Retargeting, email nurture, social proof         │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ STAGE 3: SOLUTION-AWARE                                  │
│ "I know there are consultancies that solve this"          │
│ Content: Services pages, case studies, comparison guides  │
│ Channel: Direct traffic, branded search, referrals        │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ STAGE 4: PRODUCT-AWARE                                   │
│ "I'm considering TaqHaus specifically"                    │
│ Content: About page, testimonials, pricing/engagement     │
│ Channel: Homepage, strategy call page, proposals          │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ STAGE 5: MOST-AWARE                                      │
│ "I need a reason to say YES right now"                    │
│ Content: Case studies, ROI calculators, urgency triggers  │
│ Channel: Email sequences, retargeting, direct outreach    │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Page → Stage Mapping (Website Audit Framework)

Every page on the website must serve at least one stage:

| Page | Primary Stage | Secondary Stage | Missing = Problem |
|------|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Homepage | Stage 3–4 | Stage 2 (pain points) | If homepage doesn't qualify/disqualify, it wastes pipeline |
| Services | Stage 3 | Stage 4 | If services page doesn't answer "what do I get?", prospects bounce |
| About | Stage 4 | Stage 3 | If about page doesn't build trust, they won't book |
| Case Studies | Stage 4–5 | Stage 3 | If no case studies, you have no proof |
| Blog/Insights | Stage 1–2 | Stage 3 | If no content, you have no organic discovery |
| Contact/Book | Stage 5 | Stage 4 | If booking is friction-heavy, qualified leads drop off |
| Lead Magnet | Stage 2–3 | Stage 1 | If no lead magnet, you can't nurture the 95% who aren't ready |
| Pricing/Engagement | Stage 4 | Stage 5 | If pricing is unclear, prospects ghost |

## Messaging Architecture

### The Message Hierarchy

Every page follows this hierarchy. Violating the order creates cognitive confusion:

```
1. Category Claim     → "Marketing consultancy" (What are you?)
2. Differentiation    → "Strategy + execution under one roof" (Why you?)
3. Proof              → Metrics, testimonials, case studies (Why believe you?)
4. Action             → CTA with clear next step (What do I do now?)
```

### Copy Calibration by Temperature

| Prospect Temperature | Copy Style | CTA Intensity | Example |
|---------------------|------------|---------------|---------|
| Cold (outreach, ads) | Pain-first, empathy-driven | Low — "Learn more" | "Most agencies deliver reports. We deliver revenue." |
| Warm (return visit, nurture) | Solution-forward, credibility | Medium — "See how" | "Here's how we increased conversion 22% for [client]" |
| Hot (services page, pricing) | Outcome-specific, confidence | High — "Book now" | "Book your 30-minute strategy call. No pitch. No pressure." |

### The Anti-Patterns

**Never do these on a consultancy website:**

1. **Feature dumping** — Listing 47 services without hierarchy tells the prospect you don't know what you're best at
2. **Vanity metrics** — "10,000 followers" means nothing to a CEO evaluating a $5K/mo retainer
3. **Stock photography** — Smiling diverse teams in glass offices destroy authenticity for boutique consultancies
4. **Jargon walls** — "Leverage synergistic omnichannel solutions" tells the prospect you have nothing real to say
5. **Missing CTAs** — Every section needs a logical next step. A page without CTAs is a dead end
6. **Pricing avoidance** — Showing zero context around investment scares off qualified leads and attracts price-shoppers simultaneously
7. **No objection handling** — If your FAQ doesn't address the top 3 reasons people DON'T buy, you're leaving money on the table

## Demand Generation Architecture

### The Inbound Flywheel

```
             ┌── SEO/GEO Content ──┐
             │                      │
     Blog ───┤                      ├─── Organic Traffic
             │                      │
             └── Social LinkedIn ──┘
                      │
                      ▼
              Lead Magnet Capture
             (Website Audit Tool)
                      │
                      ▼
              Email Nurture Sequence
             (3–5 emails, value-first)
                      │
                      ▼
               Strategy Call Booking
                      │
                      ▼
                  Revenue ←── Case Study ←── Loop
```

### The Outbound Supplement

Inbound alone is slow for a new consultancy. The outbound layer:

```
1. LinkedIn DMs      → "Saw your [website/campaign]. Here's a quick audit."
2. Cold Email        → Pain-point opener → asset (blog post) → CTA (strategy call)
3. Warm Referrals    → Ask existing partners for 2 introductions per quarter
4. Content Seeding   → Publish insights on LinkedIn → Tag prospects → Build authority
```

### Lead Magnet Strategy

The lead magnet is the bridge between "interested" and "in pipeline":

**Tier 1 (Low commitment):**
- Website performance snapshot (domain input → automated audit)
- Free guide/checklist download

**Tier 2 (Medium commitment):**
- Personalized Loom video audit (record a 5-min teardown of their site)
- Competitive benchmark report

**Tier 3 (High commitment):**
- 30-minute strategy call
- Full marketing audit session

**Rule**: Never skip tiers. A cold prospect won't book a strategy call. Give them a Tier 1 first.

## Competitive Moating

### Moat Architecture for Service Businesses

Service businesses can't patent their way to defensibility. Moats come from:

```
1. Positioning Moat     → "We're the only consultancy that does X + Y"
2. Process Moat         → "Our proprietary 4-step process" (documented, branded)
3. Expertise Moat       → "Certified in 12 platforms" (credentialing)
4. Relationship Moat    → "94% retention rate" (switching costs)
5. Content Moat         → "400 published articles" (SEO compound effect)
6. Data Moat            → "Benchmarks from 200+ campaigns" (insights from scale)
```

### Competitive Differentiation Framework

When auditing a website, evaluate the competitive position:

| Question | Strong Answer | Weak Answer |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| What are you? | Clear category claim in hero | Vague or missing |
| Who is this for? | Named ICPs with specific pain | "For all businesses" |
| Why you over alternatives? | Named differentiator + proof | "We're passionate about marketing" |
| Why now? | Urgency trigger or cost-of-inaction | No urgency mechanism |
| What do I do next? | Single clear CTA | Multiple competing CTAs or none |

## Sales Enablement

### Website as Sales Tool

The website must function as a 24/7 sales rep. Every page must do one of these:

1. **Qualify** — Help the right prospects self-select (ICP section, services page)
2. **Educate** — Build understanding of your approach (case studies, blog, process section)
3. **Convert** — Move them to the next step (CTAs, booking pages, lead magnets)
4. **Reassure** — Remove risk and objections (FAQ, testimonials, guarantees)

### Pre-Call Nurture Sequence

Between lead capture and strategy call, the prospect should receive:

```
Email 1 (Immediate): "Here's your [audit/guide/resource]" — deliver the promise
Email 2 (Day 2):     "One thing most businesses miss" — position expertise
Email 3 (Day 4):     "How [client] achieved [result]" — social proof
Email 4 (Day 7):     "Ready to talk?" — soft CTA to strategy call
Email 5 (Day 14):    "Quick question" — re-engagement for non-responders
```

## Revenue Attribution

### What To Measure

```
Awareness Metrics:    Organic traffic, social reach, branded search volume
Engagement Metrics:   Time on site, pages per session, scroll depth, return visits
Capture Metrics:      Lead magnet conversions, email subscribers, form fills
Pipeline Metrics:     Strategy calls booked, proposals sent, close rate
Revenue Metrics:      Client LTV, retention rate, revenue per channel
```

### The Attribution Hierarchy

1. **First-touch**: Which channel brought them in? (SEO? LinkedIn? Referral?)
2. **Multi-touch**: Which pages did they visit before converting?
3. **Last-touch**: What was the final action before booking?
4. **Revenue**: Which channel produces the highest LTV clients?

## Website Audit Checklist

When auditing a consultancy website, evaluate against these criteria:

### Hero Section
- [ ] Category clarity in under 3 seconds
- [ ] Named or implied ICP
- [ ] Single primary CTA (not competing calls to action)
- [ ] Visual hierarchy: headline > subheadline > CTA > trust signal
- [ ] Mobile-first: does it work on a phone screen?

### Buyer Journey Coverage
- [ ] Stage 1 content exists (blog, SEO articles)
- [ ] Stage 2 content exists (pain point articulation, problem education)
- [ ] Stage 3 content exists (services page, solution overview)
- [ ] Stage 4 content exists (about, testimonials, trust signals)
- [ ] Stage 5 content exists (case studies, ROI evidence, urgency triggers)

### Trust Architecture
- [ ] Real testimonials with names and context
- [ ] Defensible metrics (no made-up numbers)
- [ ] Platform/certification credentials
- [ ] Response time guarantee
- [ ] Case studies with challenge → approach → results

### Conversion Infrastructure
- [ ] Lead magnet (low-commitment capture)
- [ ] email capture mechanism
- [ ] Strategy call booking (clear, friction-free)
- [ ] FAQ that handles objections
- [ ] Multiple CTAs per page (but ONE primary per section)

### Technical SEO/GEO
- [ ] Meta titles and descriptions on every page
- [ ] Structured data (FAQ, Organization, LocalBusiness)
- [ ] Open Graph and Twitter Card tags
- [ ] Sitemap.xml
- [ ] Canonical URLs
- [ ] Schema markup for services

### Missing Pages (Common Gaps)
- [ ] Dedicated case studies page with full narratives
- [ ] Industry-specific landing pages
- [ ] Pricing/engagement transparency page
- [ ] Resource hub (guides, templates, tools)
- [ ] Careers page (signals growth and professionalism)
